Foundations reflections

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

- That I'm nowhere near as smart as I wish to be. That software development is an amazing and sophisticated language that hopefully one day I'll learn and be fluent at. That a gentle and kind soul is more important than broad shoulders.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

- A piece of the internet that I found on google which I think helps sum all my beliefs and understanding in to one. "Empathy is the ability to understand and share the feelings of others. For software developers, empathy is crucial for creating user-friendly and effective software that meets the needs of users, clients, and stakeholders. By putting themselves in others' shoes, developers can: Understand user needs and pain points Design intuitive and easy-to-use interfaces Improve communication and collaboration with team members Build trust and stronger relationships with clients Foster a positive and inclusive work environment"

What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

- That a lot of what is being taught is what I grew up being around. A lot of the teachings are embedded in Maori culture.

What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

- Other than sticking to the commitments of what I said I would do, nothing, truthfully.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

- I want to assume that it is beneficial in every way imaginable eg. Great for career development, Improves life quality, Benefits your mental health and SOOOO many more things. It just seems logical and truth is, it isn't hard.

Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

- In some ways, YES. My belief is that, if you need to learn how to treat people than you need therapy. As for the professional aspect of it all, that's where we need to learn especially for us that have a habit of pushing conversation more to the comfortable "friendly" side.
