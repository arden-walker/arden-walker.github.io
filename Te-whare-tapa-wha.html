My wellbeing plan:

My wellbeing plan is based on the Te Whare Tapa Wha model. This model was developed by Sir Mason Durie in 1982. It is a holistic approach to health and wellbeing that includes four dimensions: taha tinana (physical), taha wairua (spiritual), taha hinengaro (mental), and taha whanau (family). The model also includes whenua (land) as an important aspect of wellbeing.

Taha-Tinana (Physical Wellbeing)

-To exercise at least 45 minutes a day

-To maintain the tidiness of my house

-To eat well

-To sleep well

Taha-Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing)

-To korero with my family

-To meditate at least 10 to 20 minutes per day

-To sleep well, 8 hours per night

Taha-Hinengaro (Mental Wellbeing)

-To read at least one chapter per day

-To korero with my family

-To practice mindfulness

-To meditate 10 to 20 minutes per day

Taha-Whanau (Family Wellbeing)

-To korero with my family

-To help my siblings when they need help

-To go for a dive

-To go for whanau walks

Whenua

-To get exercise by walking through our bush and along our beautiful beaches.

-To go for a dive in our moana, get some kai (Craving cream paua)
