Learning Plan

1. What is your long term goal or career pathway:

To enter the field of software development, to design an app that will help others in their path to learning their heritage, especially in our Māori community but still for everyone. To one day take my skills back to Australia depending on job opportunities here in Aotearoa.

2. A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning:

One of my strengths is my willingness to look and think outside the box. I've always believed that sometimes the best way to solve a problem is to help someone else with their problem or to look somewhere different. Answers come from the most unexpected places if you open your mind to interpretation. - "Sometimes the best way to solve your own problems is to help someone else” - Uncle Iroh, Avatar the last airbender. My limitations - My belief that, asking for help is me being a hindrance/nuisance/an incovenience (This will take some serious work, no doubt, especially if one of my plans when stuck is to ASK OTHERS for help/advice).

3. What skills (non-technical/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy:

I would like to see my ability to communicate to others improve. I believe that a lot of the opportunities/chances that I have missed throughout my life have been due to my inability to communicate. My beliefs in not being a hindrance on others have played a major part in this and is something I need to work on.

4. A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme: - An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with others:

My friendly and adaptable nature should come in handy here. I will use this to find the space I am needed, offer assistance where I can and to identify the things I know I will struggle with. - What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you: Either look for advice/help from facilitators and other members of our cohort and to let others know of where and what I am struggling with.

5. A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way:

Ask the internet and if that yields no results then I will turn to my peers and our facilitators for guidance/advice.

6. A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team:

Guidance, advice and an open learning space. I expect the facilitators to help us identify and navigate developer issues and to help place things in to context that will grow our understanding. An added bonus is the chance to ask what may seem like a silly question - "It's better to ask a dumb question than to make a dumb mistake" - Gazza, My old boss.

7. Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things. For example, you might have whanau commitments like school drop-offs:

I have no other commitments that I know of, however, "never say never" am I right?
