Identity and Values

1. Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now:

- "Do I ever consider the ethics of a decision?" I ask myself, and after a solid thinking session, I noticed that I consider the ethics of just about every decision I make. The most recent time that comes to mind is, as I was driving, I had a perfect line to take out a possum to which I decided against. My reason? because it's a life, it never asked to be here or to be put in a position where it will be slain all over the road merely because of where it was born and what it was born as. I know that they are considered pest and are destructive to Aotearoa's environment but my heart wouldn't let me do it and also, if there's one thing I know to be ABSOLUTE! it is that mother nature is adaptable. As for how I reflect on my decision? A mental-note that comes to my mind is that I abide by my nature and have done so since I was a baby.

2. Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity:

- Growing up Maori in a small country town, the thing you're constantly surrounded by is family and Te Taiao (The Natural World) and because of this, I grew up heavily influenced by my Maori side. It consisted of things like Manaakitanga (Generosity/caring) and being that safe place for someone, even if its only for a brief moment. Tikanga (Customs/traditional values) and the value in respecting others and everything around you. Whakapapa (Lineage) knowing where I come from and where I belong to help maintain my character, my hinengaro (Mind), who I am and the values we hold. These values helped me to navigate the struggles of life throughout my time as an adolescent and are things I still hold dear even as an adult. These values (although I didn't know back then) helped me and kept me alive throughout my time in Australia. They brought me amazing memories, great experiences and even greater friends and that's something I'll forever be grateful for. As for my identity, MAORI!

5. Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why:

- My most recent job. I have a very "get it done now" approach when it comes to work and I admit, I can be very impatient BUT there's also a line of where being too patient prevents things from being done. This was my last job, everyone loved to laugh and joke (I do to) but when it's to the point that there is more laughing and joking then working, it gets old and tiring, especially because I'm a thinker and when I'm working I get to think. Another thing that made me hoha (annoyed) was how everyone would slack off and talk while the work would pile up and so, by the time the morning shift would leave, it would be me and two others left to do a hundred trucks. The resistance/tension? This was EVERYDAY. The strategy I took? I kept my head down and carried on working which bit me in the butt, I was left to do all the work while everyone saw it as an opportunity to do nothing. What strategy would I try? Tell the bosses.
